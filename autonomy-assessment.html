Definition: Autonomous IT

Autonomous IT is an operating model where systems act on defined intent and policy—detecting signals, reasoning about impact, and executing approved actions across infrastructure, applications, and services.

Reference Screen

The Autonomous IT Maturity Model

Definitions included verbatim.

Level 0 — Chatbot: The system answers questions. Humans make every decision.
Level 1 — Assistant: The system recommends. Humans approve and execute.
Level 2 — Agents: The system acts with human sign-off at every step.
Level 3 — Advanced Agents: The system executes bounded actions automatically within defined guardrails.
Level 4 — Expert Agents: The system handles multi-step workflows end-to-end; humans shift to policy and exception review.
Level 5 — Agent Ecosystems: Agents coordinate with other agents across domains with minimal human intervention.

Score interpretation (total out of 25)

  • 0–7 → Level 0–1: Foundation work needed before autonomy is viable
  • 8–13 → Level 2: Early agency in place—execution capability is the next unlock
  • 14–18 → Level 3: Bounded autonomy running—governance and scope are the next frontier
  • 19–22 → Level 4: High autonomy—focus shifts to cross-domain coordination
  • 23–25 → Level 5: Full autonomy—agent ecosystems operating at scale
